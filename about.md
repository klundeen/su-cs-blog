**CS Perspectives** is a [proposed] blog by faculty in Seattle University's Department of Computer Science. We write about algorithms, teaching, AI, and the things we argue about over coffee. The bar for a post is simple: *would this be interesting to a smart person who isn't necessarily in your subfield?*

The posts here are personal — classroom stories, technical explorations, half-formed ideas we're still chewing on. They represent the views of their authors, not official positions of the department or university.

A CS department should produce writing, not just code. Our students learn to communicate; so should we.

## Contributing

Every faculty member in the department has something worth writing about. You don't need a finished idea — just a question you find interesting, an algorithm you think is beautiful, a teaching experiment that surprised you, or an argument you want to make. We'll help shape it. The writing process is part of the point.

See [How to Contribute]({{ '/contribute/' | relative_url }}) for details, or reach out to [Kevin Lundeen](mailto:lundeenk@seattleu.edu) to get started.
