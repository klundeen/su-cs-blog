<div class="container">
  <div class="article-body" style="padding-top: 2.5rem;">
    <p><strong>CS Perspectives</strong> is a [proposed] blog by faculty in Seattle University's Department of Computer Science. We write about algorithms, teaching, AI, and the things we argue about over coffee. The bar for a post is simple: <em>would this be interesting to a smart person who isn't necessarily in your subfield?</em></p>

    <p>The posts here are personal — classroom stories, technical explorations, half-formed ideas we're still chewing on. They represent the views of their authors, not official positions of the department or university.</p>

    <p>A CS department should produce writing, not just code. Our students learn to communicate; so should we.</p>

    <h2>Contributing</h2>

    <p>Every faculty member in the department has something worth writing about. You don't need a finished idea — just a question you find interesting, an algorithm you think is beautiful, a teaching experiment that surprised you, or an argument you want to make. We'll help shape it. The writing process is part of the point.</p>

    <p>Reach out to <a href="mailto:lundeenk@seattleu.edu">Kevin Lundeen</a> to get started.</p>
