    <h1>About CS Perspectives</h1>
    <p class="subtitle">A space for Seattle University CS faculty to share ideas, reflections, and dispatches from the classroom.</p>
  </div>

<div class="container">
  <div class="article-body" style="padding-top: 2.5rem;">
    <p><strong>CS Perspectives</strong> is an informal blog by faculty in Seattle University's Department of Computer Science. We write about the things we think about — how technology works, how teaching works, and what happens when those two collide.</p>

    <p>The posts here are our own: personal reflections, classroom stories, technical explorations, and occasionally an argument about something we care about. They represent the views of their authors, not official positions of the department or university.</p>

    <p>We believe that a CS department should produce writing, not just code. Our students learn to communicate; so should we.</p>

    <h2>Contributing</h2>

    <p>CS Perspectives is open to all faculty in the Department of Computer Science. If you'd like to contribute a post, reach out to <a href="mailto:lundeenk@seattleu.edu">Kevin Lundeen</a>.</p>
